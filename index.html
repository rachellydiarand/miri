I give credit where credit is due.

I did pick up on positive things from the Memphis Jazz Orchestra. I simply have a big enough ego to say that if I didn't pick up on that stuff, I would have picked up other stuff that would have been just as good because I am still me whatever I am doing.

That said, I certainly picked up on Howard Lamb's trombone sound and style. I feel he is similar in some ways to Carl Fontana and I picked up on Carl Fontana's sound as well from just one recording of him that I had and don't have now.

Also, and perhaps even bigger than the thing I have with Howard, is Scott Thompson. He was/is a trumptet player, improviser. One of those hot jazz trumpet players with a huge, amazing sound! He was probably a couple years older than me and he was taking huge advantage of harmonic minor scale melodies in his improvising and that's certainly a mainstay in my improvising as well.

I picked up on that before I moved to Memphis. My transcriptions were focusing on that stuff and then I took some lessons from Jeff Putterman who is a guitarist who was teaching Music Theory at Berklee College of Music before he moved to Lake Oswego Oregon and started teaching at Lewis and Clark College. He showed me all the formal stuff of the Harmonic Minor scales and harmony. So I was already there, but yes, Scott drove that stuff home with me.

After all of that, I've been on my own as far as jazz improvising and trombone goes. Completely. No mentors on that front. Yes, Sting has been a huge mentor of mine, but it's not about minor scale harmony and trombone. And with him, it's not even about singing and songwriting as far as my opinion goes. I stopped "following" him in December 2023. I'm on my own like that now and winging it. It's difficult and extremely high level if you ask me.

There are other people in MJO too. I enjoy talking about them and their musical styles. Just because I am not a band musician now doesn't mean I didn't enjoy that stuff then. But I am very based in meditation and it was extreme doing that MJO gig that much. My feeling is that I was happy to participate in a giving back to the community kind of way. They need players to fill those positions and I filled a position on plenty of nights.
